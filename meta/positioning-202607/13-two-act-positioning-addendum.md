> those documents already describe it.

## The tension this resolves

On 2026-07-17 the founder raised a repositioning instinct: "Right now we're positioned for developers.
Instead, I'd like to position towards business users… especially with our Shapes." Taken literally and
immediately, that instinct collides with three things already in flight: the developer/operator beachhead
that `02-positioning.md` builds from scratch, the imminent 14-week GTM plan (`09-gtm-plan.md`), and the
demo-claim gate (`09-gtm-plan.md` §2.0) that already governs every launch pillar — under which business-facing
Shapes are the least-verified surface in the whole program (zero reference shapes shipped, zero non-developer
users, unevaled). The two-act structure below is how the instinct gets honored without breaking the gate.

## The two acts

**Act 1 (now).** Launch and market DorkOS on the existing operator/developer positioning exactly as planned:
"mission control for every coding agent you run," the Kai/Priya beachhead, the 14-week ladder in
`09-gtm-plan.md` Part 4. No Act-1 launch copy changes as a result of this addendum. Shapes exist in Act 1 as
the dev-facing Script-2 / second-visit story (`08-demo-video-scripts.md`) — introduced after launch, not as
the headline.

**Act 2 (evidence-gated).** A deliberate, later expansion of positioning toward business users and operators
broadly, riding the door the persona work already opened ("operator mentality, not technical skill," per
`AGENTS.md`). Act 2 fires only when **all three** evidence triggers are met:

1. **≥3 business-facing Shapes work end-to-end** — not prototypes; installed and used, evals green.
2. **A real non-developer user cohort exists** — actual Ikechi-persona users, not projected ones.
3. **Desktop-app install friction is effectively zero** — the primary on-ramp for a non-developer audience
   can't be a terminal command.

When the triggers clear, Act 2 gets **its own positioning review cycle** — a fresh pass through the same
calibration process that produced this corpus — rather than an edit-in-place of Act-1 copy. Business
positioning is a different narrative, not a patch to the developer one.

## Where this shows up in the existing plan

- `plans/shapes-program.md` Phase 4 states the operational rule — "No Act-1 launch-message changes," Shapes
  stays the Script-2 / second-visit story, and Act 2 "gets its own positioning review cycle (not an
  edit-in-place of Act 1 copy)"; this addendum is the decision record behind those lines, not a new
  instruction to them.
- `plans/shapes-program.md` success criterion 5 calls for an **Act-2 trigger dashboard** (even a checklist)
  so the repositioning decision stays evidence-driven — that dashboard is the accountability structure for
  the triggers above and does not exist yet.
